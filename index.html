Podcast Script

Opening Frame for Guests

This sets the tone for the entire recording and makes the transition at the end natural.

Step 1: Ask Permission to Set Context

"Do you mind if I give you a quick bit of context about the podcast before I dive into the questions?"

Step 2: Who You Are & Why

"So I run a supplement fulfillment company called On Demand Fulfillment, and we're reaching out to leaders in the marketing space because -- quite selfishly [laugh] -- I'd love to gather insights from people like you that I can share with our customers. Hopefully we can make a connection that benefits both of us. Maybe you have some clients that could use our services, and in return, we're running this podcast to give back."

Step 3: Today Is About Them + Content Promise

"Today is all about you. I'm going to ask you about five or six questions -- just a simple conversation. At the end, we're going to clip up the podcast, pick out the most interesting parts where you say really interesting things, edit them, caption them, and deliver those to you so you can share them on LinkedIn and look like an authority. That's the idea."

Step 4: Plant the Seed, No Pressure

"If I think there's a fit or if it makes sense, I can talk about what that looks like at the end, but today is about you. Does that sound fair enough?"

Gets verbal agreement that you will have a moment at the end. Zero pressure during the interview.

Podcast Script

Interview Questions by Guest Type

Three sets of questions depending on who is on the podcast. All guests get the same opener and closer. The middle questions shift based on what they do.

All Guests -- Opener

"Welcome to the podcast everyone, today we're joined by [Name] who's the [title] of [Company]. Welcome [Name] -- tell us a little about yourself and what [Company] does."

After they answer: "So who do you serve? Who's your ideal client?"

For Agency Owners (DTC Facebook / TikTok)

  1. "Are you doing anything interesting with AI in your agency right now?"
  2. "What do you think have been the biggest struggles you've had to overcome in the last 12 months?"
  3. "What sort of marketing do you run for yourselves? How do you fill your own top of funnel?"
  4. "If you could go back in a time machine and restart your journey, what would you do differently?"
  5. "If you had a magic wand and could wave it over one big problem in the business right now and solve it -- what would that be?"

For D2C Brand Owners

  1. "Walk us through how you got started. What made you decide to launch your own brand?"
  2. "What does your day-to-day actually look like? Walk us through a typical day running [brand]."
  3. "What's been the hardest part of scaling from where you started to where you are now?"
  4. "If you could go back and start over knowing what you know now, what would you do differently?"
  5. "If you had a magic wand and could solve one big problem in the business right now -- what would it be?"

For Info Sellers / Course Creators / Coaches

  1. "What inspired you to start teaching other people how to do this?"
  2. "What does a typical student look like when they come to you, and what do they look like when they come out the other side?"
  3. "What's the biggest mistake you see people make when they're trying to launch their first product?"
  4. "If you could go back and start your journey over, what would you do differently?"
  5. "If you had a magic wand and could solve one big problem in your business right now -- what would it be?"

All Guests -- Closer (On Camera)

"Thanks for watching everyone, see you on the next one."

Stop recording. Go straight into the post-podcast transition.

Podcast Script -- Strategic

Back Pocket Questions

Keep these in your back pocket. Drop them naturally into the conversation when the moment is right. They are designed to surface needs that ODF can solve -- without sounding salesy.

For Agency Owners

"When one of your campaigns really takes off for a client and the orders start pouring in -- what usually happens on the operations side? Can they keep up?"

Listen for: Fulfillment bottlenecks, stockouts, shipping delays, clients who can't scale operations as fast as the marketing scales. If they mention this, Daniel knows: "We solve exactly that."

"Have you ever had a client whose growth was limited not by the marketing but by something else entirely?"

Listen for: Supply chain issues, manufacturing delays, fulfillment errors, inventory problems. This opens the door for: "We remove that bottleneck for brands."

"Have you ever thought about building something of your own -- taking all these skills and using them to launch your own product?"

Listen for: Interest in side income, leveraging marketing skills, building equity. Sets up Transition B perfectly: "Some of our best clients are agency owners who built their own brands."

For D2C Brand Owners

"If you could double your orders tomorrow, could your operations actually handle it?"

Listen for: Hesitation, "probably not," mentions of warehouse limits, shipping delays, staffing. Daniel thinks: "We're built for that -- Luka does $1M/month with us."

"What's the one thing eating up most of your time that isn't actually growing the business?"

Listen for: Fulfillment logistics, inventory management, supplier coordination, packaging. Direct ODF fit: "We take all of that off your plate."

"How did you find your current fulfillment partner? And what would make you switch?"

Listen for: Frustration with current provider, slow shipping, errors, poor communication, feeling stuck. Classic gap selling: identify the current pain, then offer the better future state.

For Info Sellers / Course Creators

"When your students finish your program and go to actually launch a product -- what's the thing most of them get stuck on?"

Listen for: "They don't know where to get products made," "fulfillment is confusing," "finding a good manufacturer." Daniel thinks: "We're the missing piece in their curriculum."

"Do you have an ecosystem of tools and partners you recommend to your students, or do they kind of figure that out on their own?"

Listen for: No fulfillment partner in their stack, or a bad one. Opens the affiliate conversation: "We could be that recommended partner."

"What percentage of your students actually go on to launch a product and start selling? What stops the rest?"

Listen for: Low launch rate, complexity of manufacturing, not knowing where to start with fulfillment. Daniel thinks: "We get people launched in 30 days. We could dramatically improve their student success rate."
After the Recording

Post-Podcast Transitions

Three scenarios based on what the guest revealed during the interview. Pick the one that fits and go straight into it after the recording stops.

Scenario A -- Referral Partner (Agency Owner)

Use when: They run an agency with clients who could be ODF customers.

"Hey, that was amazing. Thanks so much for that. Listen, you mentioned you're working with [specific thing they said]. One of our best customers is a TikTok influencer who went from zero to $200,000 a month in 60 days. I don't know if it'd be a fit or not, but if you're coming across people like that and you refer them to us, we have a really profitable referral program. I'd love to show you what that looks like -- would you be totally against jumping on another call? No pressure."
Scenario B -- Wants to Launch Own Brand (Agency or Course Creator)

Use when: They mentioned wanting new income streams, or they have the skills to sell but haven't launched their own thing. Callback to something they said during the interview.

"You mentioned you're always looking for different streams of income because marketing is a superpower you've mastered. Some of our best clients are actually marketing agency owners who've gone off and built their own supplement brands. Completely free, no obligation -- I'd love to show you a case study of one of our best clients and how he broke down the 10 steps he used to go from zero to $200K a month in 60 days. He's never published that anywhere online. I don't know if that's a fit, but I'd love to jump on another call and walk you through it."
Scenario C -- Direct Prospect (D2C Brand Owner)

Use when: They own a supplement or DTC brand and could use fulfillment directly.

"Based on what you shared about [their specific situation], I think there might be something that could really help. We put together [specific offer -- e.g. a 30-day launch roadmap / fulfillment audit / cost comparison] that shows you exactly [specific outcome in 30 days]. Would you be against me walking you through how that works on another call?"

The offer needs a specific, tangible outcome. "We'll show you how to go from idea to first shipment in 30 days" or "We'll map out your first 5 SKUs and show you exact margins."

Why This Matters

Podcast guests are not converting because the transition is messy -- no clear frame at the start, no smooth handoff at the end, and no system to make sure the follow-up happens. This process fixes all three.

This is what we think would work better -- a revenue share model instead of lump sums. Partners earn every month their referrals stay active. Recurring income is a much stronger pitch than one-time payouts.

TierActive ReferralsCommissionActivation Bounty
