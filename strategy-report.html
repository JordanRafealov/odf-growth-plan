Objection Handling Scripts

ObjectionResponse Framework
"I'm too busy" It's 25 minutes — less than a coffee meeting. No prep needed. We can do async if that's easier (send questions, they record solo). Flexible scheduling — early morning, evening, whatever works.
"What's your audience size?" Don't compete on vanity metrics. "Our listeners are brand owners spending $50K-500K/month on DTC. It's not about downloads — it's about who's listening." Mention content repurposing: clips, social posts, SEO value.
"I don't see the value" Four value props: (1) Content they can repurpose for their own marketing, (2) Exposure to 300+ supplement brands, (3) Social clips with professional editing, (4) Relationship with ODF network.
"How many listeners?" Reframe: "We're niche — our audience is supplement brand owners and DTC marketers, not mass market. The value is who's listening, not how many." Then pivot to repurposing reach.
"Can you send more info?" Have a ready-to-send info email template: past guests, episode format (25 min, casual, Zencastr), distribution channels, what they get (clips, social assets, episode link).
"I've never been on a podcast" Low-pressure: "Most of our best episodes are with first-timers — real conversations, not rehearsed soundbites. I'll send the topics in advance so you know exactly what we'll cover."
The Conversion Engine

Podcast Framing & Business Transition

The biggest fix: how to frame the recording, plant seeds during the conversation, and transition to business after.

Current Problem (From Daniel)

"People aren't clear that the recording is happening on that call." Guests are confused about format, expectations, and what happens with the content. No framework exists for transitioning from interview to business conversation.

Pre-Record Setup Script (Before Hitting Record)

"Before we jump in — just want to set expectations. This is going to be a casual 25-minute conversation. I'll ask you about [2-3 topics]. It's recorded on Zencastr — video and audio. We handle all editing. You'll get to review everything before it goes live. If there's anything you want off the record during the conversation, just say so and we'll cut it. Sound good?"

This solves the "guests don't know they're being recorded" problem. Sets expectations, gives them control, reduces anxiety.

Transition Questions — 3 Arcs

ArcTimingQuestionsPurpose
Warm-Up Min 1-10 "What's your background?" / "How did you get into this?" / "What's working for your clients right now?" Build rapport. Establish them as the expert.
Pivot Min 10-18 "Who do you typically serve?" / "Who is your ideal client?" / "What products are your clients selling most?" Surface their clients' product ambitions. Natural bridge to supplements.
Bridge Min 18-25 "With all this marketing experience, what's stopping you from launching your own brand?" / "Have you ever considered launching your own supplement line?" The money questions from Daniel's call. Plants the seed.

Off-Air Transition Script (After Recording Stops)

"Hey, that was great. Really enjoyed the conversation. Quick thing — something you said about [specific thing they mentioned] really resonated. We actually run the fulfillment side at ODF — 300+ supplement brands, FDA/GMP compliant, brands launch in 30 days. We have a partner program where agencies like yours earn 25-35% of gross profit on every brand you refer. I'm not pitching you — just thought it might be relevant given what you shared. Would it be worth a 15-minute call to see if there's fit?"

Why "I'm not pitching you" works: It's the most effective pitch opener because it immediately lowers defenses. They just spent 25 minutes building rapport. The reference to something THEY said makes it feel organic, not scripted.
